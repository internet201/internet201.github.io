What is this?

This is a harmless site meant to warn any visitors about the dangers of phishing sites.

A phishing site may try to trick you into giving up your social security number, credit card number, or simply any information that a hacker may find useful. Also, a phishing site may attempt to plant malware on your device.

This sounds bad. How do I stop it?

There are a few ways to protect yourself from phishing attacks:

  • Companies will never ask you to do anything on your account through email.
    In the worst case, a company will put out a notice on the main website telling you if it is needed.
    It never hurts to contact the company to check first.

  • Make sure that if you are giving sensitive information, check that the website is trustworthy and authentic.
    If the website needs to deal with sensitive information, it should have a padlock on the top right corner, like so:
    If a website doesn't have such a padlock, do not give it any sensitive information.

    NOTE: Just because a website has a green padlock does not mean it's trustworthy. Click here to find out what the padlock really means. (under construction)

  • No individuals would ever reach out to you in order to give you a prize without having you give anything in return.

This may seem obvious, but it's worth saying it again and again: If it's too good to be true, it probably is.
